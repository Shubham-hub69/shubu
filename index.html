The Bests

A handpicked list of the most powerful, emotional, and unforgettable movies and anime I’ve ever watched.
Not ranked — each one is special for a different reason.

1. The House That Jack Built

A disturbing and philosophical dive into the mind of a serial killer. Jack believes his murders are “art,” forcing the viewer to question whether beauty can exist in something deeply immoral.

Throughout the film, Jack debates with Verge — a symbolic voice of morality — revealing humanity’s eternal struggle between logic, guilt, and conscience.

Watch for free

2. Takopi's Original Sin

A story where innocence meets the darkest corners of human pain.

Takopi, a cheerful alien, wants to help a girl trapped in loneliness, abuse, and trauma. But lacking the understanding of human suffering, his attempts create tragic consequences. It’s a heartbreaking reminder that true happiness can’t exist without recognizing sadness.

3. Violet Evergarden

Some of my favorite quotes from this masterpiece:

• “Letters are something you can hold on to. They make you feel like the person you love is still beside you.”
• “When someone says they love you, they aren’t commanding you—they’re wishing for your happiness.”
• “The stars are beautiful because they don’t know how lonely they are.”

4. Made in Abyss

A deceptively cute world hiding unimaginable horror. The deeper the characters descend into the Abyss, the darker and more disturbing the truth becomes.

The Boys

A brutal and unfiltered take on superheroes. In this world, the “heroes” are the real monsters — corrupt, violent, and untouchable — while normal humans fight back with nothing but rage and desperation. A dark, shocking, and painfully realistic reflection of power and its abuse.
